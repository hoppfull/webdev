Welcome to my F# "tutorial"!
The idea is not to keep a comprehensive tutorial. It's a sort of cheat sheet where I go through most of the most useful stuff in F#. If you're reading this and already good with programming, then this might be useful to you. I personally think the setup-section could be useful to anyone working with F#.

I will happily explain something if it doesn't require a huge explaination, but I will not go into details if it'll end up cluttering up my description of a subject with explicit details.
